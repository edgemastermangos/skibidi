About Naawi-Oodena

Naawi-Oodena is an innovative urban development project located in Winnipeg, Manitoba. Developed through a unique partnership between Treaty One First Nations and Canada Lands Company, the project embodies a vision of reconciliation, cultural respect, and community-led planning.

The name "Naawi-Oodena" means "center of the heart and community" in Ojibwe and Anishinaabemowin, highlighting the project's focus on creating a vibrant, welcoming neighborhood that honors Indigenous heritage while embracing sustainable urban living.

Partners and Collaborative Vision

The collaboration between Treaty One First Nations and Canada Lands Company represents a historic effort to blend Indigenous perspectives with contemporary urban development. Treaty One brings deep cultural knowledge and community values, while Canada Lands Company contributes expertise in real estate development and land management.

Together, they aim to create a community that is inclusive, resilient, and rooted in Indigenous identity, providing opportunities for economic growth, education, and cultural expression.

Urban Planning and Design

Naawi-Oodena’s design emphasizes sustainable environmental practices, incorporating green spaces, energy-efficient buildings, and pedestrian-friendly infrastructure. The development promotes active and healthy lifestyles through accessible parks, walking paths, and recreational facilities.

Indigenous culture is deeply embedded in the design elements, from architectural styles to the naming of streets and public spaces, fostering a strong sense of place and identity for residents and visitors alike.

Community Features

Types of Housing

The community plan includes a variety of housing options to meet diverse needs, including student housing designed to support academic life and intergenerational housing that fosters family and cultural continuity across different age groups.

Public and Cultural Spaces

Key public and cultural spaces include community gathering areas, performance venues, and Indigenous cultural centers. These spaces support ceremonies, celebrations, and educational activities that keep Indigenous traditions alive and accessible.

Recreational Facilities

Recreational amenities include playgrounds, sports fields, walking trails, and multi-use community centers. These facilities encourage active, healthy lifestyles and provide venues for social interaction and wellness programming.

Indigenous Culture in Design

The development integrates Indigenous culture in both design and naming conventions. Architectural elements reflect traditional styles and symbolism, while street and park names honor Indigenous languages, leaders, and stories, creating a living cultural landscape.

Historical Background

Previous Military Base

The site was formerly occupied by Kapyong Barracks, a Canadian military base that was officially closed in June 2004. The base played a significant role in local and national military history.

Federal Government Announcement

The federal government’s closure of the base marked the beginning of a lengthy transition period during which Treaty One First Nations worked towards reclaiming the land for Indigenous community use.

Land Control Timeline

It took approximately 16 years of legal advocacy and negotiation for Treaty One First Nations to achieve legal control over the lands, representing a landmark victory for Indigenous land rights in Canada.

Significance of Legal Battle

This legal battle set an important precedent for Indigenous communities nationwide, empowering other First Nations in their efforts to assert rights and sovereignty over traditional territories.

Future Outlook

Naawi-Oodena is envisioned as a model for Indigenous-led urban development that balances cultural integrity, environmental sustainability, and economic opportunity. With ownership extending until 2040, the project aims to foster a thriving, resilient community that inspires similar initiatives across Canada.
